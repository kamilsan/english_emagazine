The greatest comeback in the history of football

When a football fan thinks of a great comeback, some matches come to his mind – Manchester United with two goals in added time leading to 2-1 victory over Bayern Munich in Champions League final in 1999, Liverpool coming from 0-3 deficit at half-time to win on penalties against AC Milan due to famous „Dudek dance” in CL final in 2005.

This year Barcelona made their contribution to history of football.

1st match, 14th of February, Parc des Princes

PSG absolutely destroyed Barcelona. Barcelona’s midfield didn’t exist. Literally all 50/50 challenges were won by PSG players. Hopeless match, 4-0 (Di Maria’s brace, one goal by Draxler and Cavani) loss, no away goals (they are crucial when aggregate result is equal). Chances of advancement into the round for Barcelona? 0% – that is UEFA prediction.

Highlights from first match

Impossible does not exist.

2nd match, 8th of March, Camp Nou

Refurbished Barҫa (playing in 3-4-3 instead of traditional 4-3-3) starts the game with a quick goal by Luis Suarez in 3’, 40th minute – Iniesta tries backheel pass across the goal, luckily for Blaugrana the ball is deflected by Kurzawa, own goal, 2-0. Half-time. Hope increases.

49’ – Neymar runs into penalty box, Meunier slides, Neymar stumbles over Meunier’s head, at first referee decides not to give a penalty for the foul, but after the consultation with the assistant he changes his mind. Messi steps up for penalty, it’s 3-0. 62nd minute, Kurzawa heads the ball towards Cavani, he smashes the ball with the outside of his foot, under the crossbar, 3-1, looks like game over.

5 minutes + added time to go while scoring 3 goals is required to advance? Let’s see.

88’ Neymar goes for a free kick, top bins, 4-1. 90th minute – Suarez dives and is fouled at the same time, another controversy but Deniz Aytekin decides to give a penalty. Neymar steps up and it is 5-1. 95th minute – Neymar’s desperate cross, Sergi Roberto taps the ball. 6-1. Barça goes into the quarter-final.

Ecstasy.
